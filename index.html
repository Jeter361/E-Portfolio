About Me

From breaking things as a kid to fixing them as an intern, I’ve always been curious about how technology works.

I was born and raised in Norfolk, Virginia, where my passion for technology started at a young age. While playing games online, I ran into modders, and that experience sparked my interest in cybersecurity. At 13, I attended the GenCyber summer camp at Old Dominion University — the youngest participant there — and it further fueled my curiosity about computers and security.

I started building and selling gaming PCs, which gave me both hands-on technical experience and an entrepreneurial mindset. Outside of tech, I stayed active playing varsity baseball and diving at Maury High School.

Today, I’m pursuing a Cybersecurity degree at ODU while working in IT on campus. I’ve gained practical experience fixing a wide range of problems on Windows machines. I’ve seen more blue screens than I can count and replaced even more CMOS batteries, but those challenges taught me how to troubleshoot under pressure and find creative solutions.

My battlestation — where a lot of experiments begin.

Introduction

About the Department

The Batten College of Engineering & Technology supports hundreds of students and faculty who rely on computer labs, classroom technology, and specialized software every day. The IT team in Kaufman Hall keeps these systems secure, updated, and ready for use — from general-purpose labs to faculty workstations and teaching spaces.

My Role

As an Engineering Desktop Support Technician, I helped maintain and deploy the Windows systems used across the college. My work included imaging lab computers, troubleshooting hardware and software issues, performing upgrades and repairs, and assisting faculty and staff through our ticketing system.

Key Responsibilities

Lab Deployment & Imaging

Imaged and configured Windows lab machines, joined systems to the domain, enrolled them in Intune, and verified software, drivers, and classroom readiness before the semester.

Hardware Troubleshooting

Opened desktops to diagnose RAM, SSD, and motherboard issues; replaced failing components; and tested systems to ensure they were stable before returning them to service.

Cable Management & Workstations

Built clean, organized workstations for faculty and labs, including monitor, dock, and peripheral cabling to keep setups easy to use and maintain.

Equipment Delivery & Storage

Loaded carts with computers and monitors for lab builds, delivered and installed equipment on-site, and organized storage to prepare devices for future deployments.

Responsibilities in Action

A look at the spaces, hardware, and small details that keep engineering labs running.

Skills & Learning

How this internship grew both my technical depth and professional skills.

Critical Thinking & Problem Solving

I frequently had to isolate the root cause of issues across hardware, software, and networking. From blue screens to failed enrollments, I learned to break problems down into layers, test hypotheses, and confirm fixes before returning machines to service.

Career & Self-Development

This role pushed me to keep learning on my own — reading documentation, asking questions, and experimenting safely. I became more confident working independently, managing my time, and seeking feedback from supervisors to keep improving.

Technical Skills Strengthened

PowerShell Software Packaging & Deployment Microsoft Intune ServiceNow Ticketing Networking Fundamentals

Challenges & How I Overcame Them

Devices Not Enrolling or Syncing Correctly

Some systems wouldn’t show up or sync in Intune. I used PowerShell and built-in troubleshooting steps to re-run enrollment, clear old records, manually trigger syncs, and review logs to confirm that policies were being applied.

Unstable or Failing Lab Machines

Certain lab computers crashed or wouldn’t boot. I opened systems, reseated components, tested RAM and drives, and used imaging tools to deploy clean builds, learning how hardware, drivers, and software all interact.

Balancing Tickets, Projects, and Deadlines

During busy periods I juggled lab setups, individual tickets, and ongoing projects. I learned to prioritize based on impact, communicate status updates, and break work into manageable steps so critical classrooms were ready on time.

Impact & Reflection

Impact on the Department

Ensured labs were ready for classes, reduced downtime from hardware and software issues, improved workstation setups, and supported faculty and staff with reliable technical assistance.

Impact on My Career Path

Reinforced my interest in cybersecurity and system management, built confidence with enterprise tools, and confirmed my goal of pursuing a cybersecurity career with a strong technical foundation.

This internship gave me a deeper understanding of how technology supports education at scale. From lab setup to hardware repair to full system deployment, every task helped me grow into a more capable, confident, and adaptable IT professional.
